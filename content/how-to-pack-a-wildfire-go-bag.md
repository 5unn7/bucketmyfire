## What a go-bag is for

A go-bag is the kit you grab on the way out the door. Its whole job is to let you leave in minutes without thinking, and to keep your household going for about three days away from home.

Pack one bag per person. Keep each one light enough that the person carrying it can actually carry it, including kids. Store them by the door or in the car, not buried in a closet. The best kit in the world is useless if you have to dig for it while the sky turns orange.

Build it once, then check it twice a year. Fire season and the time change make easy reminders.

## The core kit, per person

Start with the basics every person needs for three days:

- Water for three days, plus a refillable bottle.
- Food that needs no cooking: bars, nuts, dried fruit, tinned food and a small opener.
- A change of clothes in cotton or wool, plus a warm layer and rain shell.
- Sturdy closed shoes. Smoke and embers are no place for sandals.
- An N95-style mask to cut smoke, and a pair of work gloves.
- A flashlight or headlamp with spare batteries.
- A small first-aid kit and any everyday items like glasses or contact supplies.

## The things you cannot replace

These are the items a store cannot sell you on the road. Pack them first, and keep them together in a waterproof folder or bag.

- All prescription medications, plus a written list of doses. Pack a week if you can.
- Identity documents: ID, passports, birth certificates, insurance papers.
- A bit of cash in small bills. Card machines fail when the power does.
- A backup of important files and photos on a small drive or in the cloud.
- A printed list of phone numbers, in case your phone dies.

A phone is your map, radio, and contact list, so keep it alive. Pack a power bank, a wall charger, and a car charger. Charge the power bank when you check the kit.

## Pack for the whole household

A household is more than the adults. Walk through it room by room and pack for everyone in it.

**Children.** A comfort item, a few quiet activities, diapers and formula if that is your stage, and a recent photo of each child in case you are separated.

**Older adults and anyone with medical needs.** Extra medication, mobility aids, hearing-aid batteries, and a written summary of conditions and contacts.

**Pets.** Food and water for three days, a bowl, a leash, a carrier, a litter option, and any medication. Add a current photo of you with the pet. It proves the pet is yours if you get separated at a shelter.

## Keep it current

A go-bag is not a one-time job. Twice a year, open every bag and:

- Swap the water and rotate the food before it expires.
- Replace dead batteries and re-charge the power bank.
- Update medications, sizes, and any documents that changed.
- Re-check the kids' clothes still fit. They will not in a year.

Fifteen minutes twice a year keeps the bag ready for the one day you need it. Pair it with a simple evacuation plan and a cleared yard, and you have done the work that matters most.
