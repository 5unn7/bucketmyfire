# bucketmyfire → a million-dollar brand (apparel-led)

> Approved 2026-06-08. North-star strategy. Supersedes the lifestyle framing in `BUSINESS-PLAN.md`.

## Context

The question: *if you were to make this a fully fledged million-dollar brand, what would you do?*

**The reframe that defines this plan:** bucketmyfire is **not a game with merch attached. It is a cause-driven apparel/lifestyle brand, and the game is its marketing engine.** The free 3D game is the most immersive brand ad ever built — a viral, zero-friction experience running on a ~100M-visitor platform — whose job is to build an audience of people who *feel the fight against wildfire* and want to **wear it**. The apparel label is the business. The game, the Steam title, and web ads are funnels and secondary revenue that feed it.

**Why this can actually reach $1M (the honest math up front).** A $1M *apparel brand* is not millions of one-time players buying a tee once. It is **tens of thousands of loyal "fire family" members buying 2–3× a year.** ~25k orders/yr at a ~$40 blended AOV = ~$1M revenue; even 10k true fans at 2–3 buys/yr × $40 gets you to $800k–1.2M. The game's reach (millions of free plays) converts a fraction to first purchase, and the **community + cause** turns those into repeat buyers. That repeat loop — not raw traffic — is the engine. POD margins alone won't build a *brand* economically, which is why the plan graduates proven designs to **owned inventory**.

**What you already have (the asset is real and rare):**
- A distinctive ~8.5/10 real-3D game that *generates an emotion* (the fight) — the hardest part of any brand.
- A **locked brand spine**: "Fight the fire" · "Wear the fight." · two-register ember/cockpit system · Bell-heli + bucket-drop iconography · northern-boreal Saskatchewan world. Already an apparel aesthetic, not just a logo.
- A **live Medusa storefront** at `shop.bucketmyfire.com`, Printful wired (designs in progress), with three in-game CTAs already pointing into it.
- A real audience-builder: live leaderboard, Daily Burn + streaks + Open Patrol endless mode, image share-card ("Beat my score").

**What's missing for an apparel brand (the gaps this plan closes):**
- **No owned audience.** No email/SMS list. Every player, share, and win is a lost contact — fatal for a drop-driven apparel brand whose lifeblood is "announce, sell out, repeat."
- **No brand identity beyond the game.** A $1M label needs designs people wear *without knowing the game*.
- **No cause spine wired in.** The wildfire-relief pledge — the brand's soul and its PR engine — exists in docs, not on products.
- **No funnel analytics, no content engine, no real product line.**

**Decisions locked:** north-star ambition; **merch IS the brand** (game = marketing engine); **graduate POD → owned label**; solo owner **+ paid help**.

## Engine A — The apparel brand (the business)

### A1. Build a real apparel identity, not game merch
- **Hire an apparel-focused brand/art director — the #1 paid hire.** A clothing identity that reads as a brand someone wears even if they never played the game: boreal/utility/workwear silhouettes, the ember-vs-cockpit palette, patch/badge iconography, the Bell + bucket-drop mark, callsign/squadron motifs.
- **The test for every design:** would someone wear this with no idea it's from a game? If no, it's merch, not brand. (Source the world from `DESIGN.md` + `theme.ts`; use the `bmf-art` skill.)
- **Verbatim spine (em-dash-free, ≤8 words, primal):** "Fight the fire." · "Wear the fight." · "The fires keep coming."

### A2. The cause is the soul (and the PR engine)
- **Pledge a real, named % of every sale to Canadian wildfire relief / firefighter funds.** Print it on the product, the site, and a public live counter ("$X raised for the fight"). The Patagonia/Tentree playbook: makes the apparel *mean* something, earns durable free press, lifts conversion and loyalty. Must be genuine and transparent.

### A3. Product ladder (community → core → margin)
- **Entry / community ($8–18):** patches, enamel pins, stickers, beanies — cheap "fire family" identity objects.
- **Core ($24–35):** the "Wear the fight." tees + caps. Everyday volume.
- **Premium / margin ($45–90):** heavyweight hoodies, workwear, outerwear — where owned-inventory margin lives.

### A4. Validate on POD, graduate winners to an owned label
- Test designs print-on-demand on the live Medusa store (no inventory risk). When a design proves sell-through, **move it to small-batch owned production** (net jumps ~$10–15 POD → ~$25–40 owned on a hoodie, plus quality, custom labels, cause-story packaging). **Only graduate proven winners.**

### A5. Drops, not a catalog
- Limited, time-boxed, themed **drops** (seasonal, wildfire-awareness dates, in-game-event-tied, province series). Scarcity + story + community countdown. Each drop is also content and a reason to email the list.

### A6. Channels (own it first, then expand)
- **Home:** the owned store. **Expand:** Instagram / TikTok Shop, Etsy (patches/pins discovery), **markets & pop-ups** at Saskatchewan/Canada outdoor + firefighter events, then **wholesale** to outdoor/firefighter retailers.

## Engine B — The game as marketing engine (reach + audience, $0 CAC)

- **Capture the audience you currently throw away — highest priority.** Email/SMS capture on the win screen, share flow, and shop ("Get the drops + back the fight"). The old `submitLead`/leads table was removed with `shop.html` — this is a fresh build.
- **Ship the CrazyGames SDK build** (the ~90–125M-visitor faucet) + GameDistribution / GameMonetize / itch / Newgrounds. Decline Poki. Ad revenue is a bonus; reach is the point.
- **Steam premium ($12–15) = reach + legitimacy + a secondary revenue line.** Widens the audience and adds cultural weight; province expansion (already in `maps/<region>/`) is game content + brand expansion. Page up; #1 CTA = join the list / see the drop, Wishlist second.
- **Point existing CTAs at the brand, and instrument them.** `storeLink.ts` now tags `?utm_campaign=win|home-rail|title` (shipped). `shareCard.ts` gets a brand/drop CTA line. Add a privacy-first event funnel (extend `telemetry/`): `game_start → win → share_click → store_click → email_submit → purchase`.

## The credible $1M math (apparel-led)

- **Engine (apparel):** ~25k orders/yr × ~$40 AOV = **~$1M**; or **10k loyal fans × 2–3 buys/yr × $40 = $0.8–1.2M.** Contribution ~30% POD, ~40%+ owned. Reached through community + repeat, not raw traffic.
- **Funnel revenue (secondary):** web ads at 1–5M plays/mo ≈ **$50–250k** cumulative; Steam at 50k–200k copies × ~$13 × ~55% ≈ **$350k–1.4M** if it lands — both fund and feed the apparel brand.
- **Cause:** not revenue — the multiplier on conversion, loyalty, and free press.

**The honest shape:** apparel is the $1M target; the game/Steam/ads are the $0-CAC audience machine and a revenue cushion. Upside is a real label; downside is protected by the game's own monetization.

## Risks / honest caveats

- **Apparel is operationally heavier than a game** (inventory, fulfillment, returns, cash in stock). Mitigation: POD-validate, graduate only proven winners.
- **"Game merch" rarely becomes a real apparel brand.** The bar is "wearable without the game" — hence the art-director hire first.
- **The cause must be sincere** — real %, named fund, public counter — or it backfires.
- **The game must stay good and keep reaching people.** It's the $0-CAC engine.
- **Community is slow to build and easy to fake.** Loyalty is earned over many drops with a real founder voice.

## Verification (the apparel-brand scoreboard, weekly)

- **Owned audience (#1 metric):** email/SMS list size + growth + open/click rate.
- **Funnel:** plays/mo → store-click % → first-purchase % → repeat-purchase %. *Targets: store-click→buy ≥1%, repeat rate toward 30%+.*
- **Brand economics:** AOV, contribution margin, **drop sell-through %** (the gate for graduating to owned inventory).
- **Cause:** $ raised on the public counter + first earned-media hit.
- **Reach engines:** CrazyGames live + plays trending up; ≥1 content piece/week; Steam wishlists building.
