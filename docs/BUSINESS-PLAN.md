# bucketmyfire — Business Plan (Solo / Bootstrapped)

> Generated from a fact-checked, multi-source research pass (market, competitors, ad/portal
> monetization, print-on-demand merch, distribution/growth, unit economics + legal). Every
> concrete number is sourced or explicitly marked `[ESTIMATE]`. Owner profile assumed:
> **solo, lifestyle, bootstrapped (~$0 budget), free consumer game + POD merch.**

## The honest take — is this a viable lifestyle business, and what's the realistic money ceiling?

Yes, as a **near-zero-cost lifestyle side-project with real merch upside — no, as a job replacement in Year 1.** Your fixed run cost is a rounding error (~CAD $20-30/yr for the domain; GitHub Pages, Cloudflare, and Supabase are all free at your data scale [supabase.com/pricing](https://supabase.com/pricing)), so there is zero survival pressure and the downside is capped at your time. But the revenue ceiling is sobering and you should plan for it without flinching: web-game ad economics are thin (a real solo dev earned **€556.92 across 451,327 CrazyGames plays = €1.23 per 1,000 plays**, net negative after costs/taxes [donislawdev.com](https://donislawdev.com/earnings-and-statistics-from-my-8-games-android-ios-webgl/)), and the "realistic first-web-game" figure of **$500-$3,000/mo** is a single Poki-sourced soft estimate that only holds at ~0.3-3M monthly sessions [indiegamebusiness.com](https://indiegamebusiness.com/web-gaming-for-indie-developers/). The corrected, honest Year-1 expectation is roughly **break-even to ~CAD $1,000 net in a base case, ~CAD $8,000-11,000 if you get a genuine viral spike + a working merch funnel** [ESTIMATE]. The thing that makes this worth doing is the unusual alignment: a procedurally-distinctive 3D game in an under-served niche, themed on a live news cycle (Canada's 2nd-worst-ever 2025 fire season, the largest fire near Candle Lake SK — *a named location on your own map* [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Canadian_wildfires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Canadian_wildfires)), with merch as a genuine second pillar where one hoodie sale (~$15-21 net) beats thousands of ad impressions. Treat it as a brand-building lifestyle asset that compounds, not a money printer.

## Positioning & wedge

**The one sentence:** *"bucketmyfire — the real-3D wildfire helicopter game about a real place: scoop from Saskatchewan's lakes, drop on spreading fires, save the town."*

**The wedge to own:** not "best flight physics" (an unwinnable arms race against studios) but **"real-3D + a real Canadian place + a real cause."** The web helicopter/firefighting category is proven-but-shallow — the flagship competitors (Poki's *Hero Rescue*, CrazyGames' *Fire Helicopter* / *Water vs Fire*) are Flappy-Bird-grade arcade clones with no momentum physics, no slung bucket, no geography [poki.com/en/g/hero-rescue](https://poki.com/en/g/hero-rescue); [crazygames.com/t/helicopter](https://www.crazygames.com/t/helicopter). The only true 3D web flight sim, GeoFS, is a generic global airliner sim with no fire mission and a steep learning curve, monetizing at €9.99/yr HD [geo-fs.com/pages/hd.php](https://www.geo-fs.com/pages/hd.php). **Nobody owns the casual-but-real-feeling, geographically-rooted middle.** That Saskatchewan/Canada civic identity is the one asset a clone farm cannot copy, and it triples as the merch brand and the PR hook. Physics is your *proof*, not your *pitch*.

## Audience & market

- **The faucet is enormous and pre-built.** Poki: **~100M monthly players, 1B+ plays/month (first crossed June 2025), 625M unique players in 2025** [techfundingnews.com](https://techfundingnews.com/browser-gaming-website-poki-won-big-at-the-dutch-game-awards-celebrating-hitting-1-billion-monthly-plays/). CrazyGames: **~90-125M monthly visits (Similarweb, early 2026; volatile month-to-month — 124.7M Mar, 93.1M Apr)**, US-led, avg session ~15-30 min [similarweb.com/website/crazygames.com](https://www.similarweb.com/website/crazygames.com/). You do not build this audience — you get accepted into it.
- **The demographic is your merch buyer.** Core web-game audience is 18-34, roughly gender-balanced (CrazyGames 51.65% M / 48.35% F, largest age 18-24; Poki 45.4% M / 54.6% F, largest age 25-34) [gamespress.com](https://www.gamespress.com/Browser-game-platform-CrazyGames-releases-extensive-analysis-of-US-bro); [similarweb.com/website/poki.com](https://www.similarweb.com/website/poki.com/). They play on locked-down devices (school/work browsers) and have disposable income and meme/merch affinity — the right buyer for a $45 hoodie.
- **Discovery is SEO/organic, which is perfect for $0 budget.** Search is the #2 traffic source for the portals (Semrush: ~37% google.com for CrazyGames, April 2026; Similarweb shows organic #2) [similarweb.com/website/crazygames.com](https://www.similarweb.com/website/crazygames.com/). A clean title and good metadata generate free, compounding traffic.
- **The niche has real traction.** The closest analog, *3D Flight Simulator*, has **43,286 rating votes on CrazyGames** (verified fact); since votes are a small single-digit % of plays, that implies several million+ lifetime plays for one niche flight title [crazygames.com/game/3d-flight-simulator](https://www.crazygames.com/game/3d-flight-simulator). Breakout web ceilings (Stickman Hook 574M, Monkey Mart 300M, Level Devil 83M lifetime) are the *top*, not the median [gamedeveloper.com](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/the-huge-hidden-web-game-market-no-one-talks-about-and-how-to-get-in-).
- **Realistic traffic ceiling [ESTIMATE]:** "good but not viral" on a portal ≈ **50k-300k plays/month**; "breakout niche hit" ≈ **1-5M plays/month** (derived from the 43k-vote analog vs the 83-574M lifetime top tier). Set your plan to the 50k-300k band, treat breakout as upside.
- **The seasonality angle is a gift — use it.** Wildfire search/news/emotion spikes **June-September** every year, and the underlying years are record-setting: 2025 was Canada's **2nd-worst ever (~8.3M ha / ~83,000 km² burned, 85,000 evacuated, largest fire ~1.4M acres near Candle Lake SK)** [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Canadian_wildfires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Canadian_wildfires); US 2026 is already at **195% of its 10-yr average by May 31 (2.41M acres, 30,588 fires)** [insideclimatenews.org](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/27032026/us-wildfires-already-setting-records-this-year/); Canada launched a **national aerial-firefighting fleet (incl. 5 heavy-lift helicopters) in May 2026** [lethbridgeherald.com](https://lethbridgeherald.com/news/national-news/2026/05/25/ottawa-launches-national-aerial-firefighting-fleet-for-2026-wildfire-season/). Time launches and merch drops to fire season; expect lumpy, not flat, demand.
- **Why merch is a first-class pillar, not an afterthought:** gaming merchandise is a **~$31.7B (2025) market growing ~20%/yr, with apparel the single largest segment (~39%)** [gminsights.com]. Apparel is wearable, the wildfire/SK aesthetic is genuinely ownable, and POD removes all inventory risk.

## Revenue model — three pillars with real per-1000-player math

### (a) Ads / portals — the passive engine (weak alone)

**Rank of channels for a solo dev:**

1. **CrazyGames — your launch portal.** No required exclusivity (an *optional* 2-month launch exclusivity gives **+50% revenue-share uplift**); accepts heavier WebGL/3D; €100 min payout, monthly via Tipalti (~targets the 10th of the following month) [docs.crazygames.com/requirements/intro](https://docs.crazygames.com/requirements/intro/); [docs.crazygames.com/payouts](https://docs.crazygames.com/payouts/). Note: its SDK **forbids third-party ad networks** on the CrazyGames-hosted build, so keep that build separate from your bucketmyfire.com build.
2. **Poki — biggest faucet, but a trap to rush.** **50% rev-share on traffic Poki sends you, 100% on traffic you bring** (search/social/your domain) — but **5-year web-only exclusivity by default**, which *also captures Discord Activities + YouTube Playables, and would likely stop you running ads on bucketmyfire.com itself* [sdk.poki.com/deals.html](https://sdk.poki.com/deals.html); [developers.poki.com/guide/monetization](https://developers.poki.com/guide/monetization). Their **<8MB initial-download requirement** is a real fit problem for a Three.js sim with glTF/audio assets [sdk.poki.com/new-requirements.html](https://sdk.poki.com/new-requirements.html). **Decline the exclusive**; revisit Poki only non-exclusively after CrazyGames proves your numbers.
3. **GameDistribution / GameMonetize — set-and-forget volume fillers.** GameDistribution: **33% of *net* revenue**, €100 threshold, ~60-day payout [static.gamedistribution.com/terms/developer.html](https://static.gamedistribution.com/terms/developer.html). GameMonetize: **45% dev share** (the "90%" only if you self-publish on your own portal too), NET-30 [gamemonetize.com/developers](https://gamemonetize.com/developers). Stack these *only if non-exclusive*; never alongside a Poki exclusive.
4. **Self-hosted (bucketmyfire.com) via AppLixir/AdinPlay/Venatus rewarded video** — captures your 100%-yours direct traffic.

**Realistic RPM — use the conservative number, not the headline.** The often-quoted $1.50-$3 (or in-app $16 rewarded) figures are optimistic/in-app. Plan on a blended **web Session RPM of ~$1.00-$1.70 per 1,000 sessions** (real AdPushup worked example: $5 eCPM × 60% fill = $1.00; $4.50 eCPM × 85% fill = $1.70) [adpushup.com/blog/HTML5-game-ads](https://www.adpushup.com/blog/HTML5-game-ads/), reality-checked by the donislawdev €1.23/1k anchor. Rewarded video earns more per impression but a 3D game serves few impressions/session.

**Ad revenue at scale (corrected, conservative):**

| Monthly plays | @ ~$1.00 RPM | @ ~$1.50 RPM | @ ~$2.50 RPM (US-skewed + rewarded) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100,000 | ~$100/mo | ~$150/mo | ~$250/mo |
| 1,000,000 | ~$1,000/mo | ~$1,500/mo | ~$2,500/mo |

*This is exactly why ads can't carry the title — and why merch is the real pillar.*

### (b) Merch / POD — the profit pillar

**Provider + storefront pick: Fourthwall, as both POD *and* storefront.** It's **$0/mo, you keep 100% of your markup** (only deductions are catalog base cost + 2.9% + $0.30 USD processing), and crucially it acts as **Merchant of Record — it registers, collects, and remits US sales tax / VAT for you** [fourthwall.com/faq](https://fourthwall.com/faq). That eliminates the single biggest solo admin nightmare. Printify is roughly cost-equivalent on the free plan (Gildan 18500 hoodie "From $21.58" vs Fourthwall $21.95) but needs a paid Shopify (~US$29/mo) to actually sell — so Fourthwall wins for a bootstrapper until you do real volume [printify.com/blog/most-profitable-print-on-demand-products](https://printify.com/blog/most-profitable-print-on-demand-products/). Point **shop.bucketmyfire.com** (a Cloudflare CNAME, trivial given your DNS) at it so the store looks first-party.

**Per-unit margin (Fourthwall, verified base costs):**

| Product | Sell price | Net profit/unit |
|---|---|---|
| Dad hat ($15.65 base) | $29.99 | **~$13.17 USD** (~$10-13 after embroidery stitch count) |
| T-shirt ($9.25 base) | $24.99 | **~$14.72 USD** |
| Hoodie ($21.95 base) | $44.99 | **~$21.44 USD** |

Sources: [fourthwall.com/print-on-demand](https://fourthwall.com/print-on-demand); corroborated by [merchtitans POD hoodie guide](https://merchtitans.com/blog/print-on-demand-hoodies-guide) ($8-22 profit/hoodie at $45-65, $49.99 sweet spot) and [printful margin guide](https://www.printful.com/blog/what-is-a-good-profit-margin-for-print-on-demand) (~$10 net at $41 tee = 32%).

**Realistic conversion — the brutal part.** Engaged *creator* audiences convert at **0.5-2%** [mediacube.io](https://mediacube.io/en-US/blog/merch-maker-for-youtubers-best-unique-items-for-your-subscribers). Your traffic is anonymous, one-session, no parasocial bond, so plan for the **bottom of or below that range: 0.1-0.3%** [ESTIMATE — judgment, not a sourced multiplier].

**Per-1,000-player merch math [ESTIMATE]:**
- **Realistic (0.2% conv, ~$15 blended net):** 2 buyers × $15 = **~$30 USD / ~$41 CAD per 1,000 players.**
- **Optimistic (0.5% conv, store on the win screen, ~$17 net):** 5 buyers × $17 = **~$85 USD / ~$115 CAD per 1,000 players.**

**This already beats banner ads (~$1-5 per 1,000 players) at the same traffic** [tenjin.com](https://tenjin.com/blog/ad-monetization-benchmark-report-2025-ecpm-ad-revenue/) — *if* you build the funnel.

**The funnel from game → shop (this is the whole game):** Alto's Adventure earned **60% of annual merch revenue in the 30 days after launching an *in-game* store, with 75% of net-new sales through it** — "the closer the point of purchase is to the point of engagement, the more likely people buy" [medium.com/shopify-gaming](https://medium.com/shopify-gaming/how-this-indie-game-made-60-of-its-annual-merch-revenue-in-30-days-e12f4951460f). So: a **"Bucket Pilot Store" button on the win/debrief screen** (peak emotion), reusing your `ui/theme.ts` tokens, opening shop.bucketmyfire.com in a new tab — likely worth more than all external marketing combined. Players who spend $1-5 on digital will pay $50+ for physical merch from a world they love, so **don't underprice** (hoodie $44.99, hat $29.99).

### (c) IAP / supporter / premium — the low-maintenance pick

**Skip custom IAP in Year 1.** F2P IAP converts at only **1-4% of *engaged* users (2-3% typical)** [konvoy.vc](https://www.konvoy.vc/newsletters/freemium-conversion-2-4-in-gaming), and your game is unauthenticated, client-side, no accounts, no native billing — realistic web IAP is well under 1% and nets **tens of dollars/month** after fees while adding refund/chargeback/tax overhead. The low-maintenance pick is a **single Ko-fi "Buy me a coffee" tip button (0% on tips, pay-what-you-want)** plus an **itch.io "name your price" ($0 minimum)** page as a second storefront — browser games get ~37-44% play-through there vs 6-10% for downloads, and you keep ~90-100% [itch.io/docs/creators/pricing](https://itch.io/docs/creators/pricing); [howtomarketagame.com](https://howtomarketagame.com/2025/05/12/benchmark-itch-io-traffic/). *(Verify itch.io browser play doesn't breach a Poki exclusive — another reason to decline Poki's exclusive.)* Revisit cosmetic IAP via a Merchant-of-Record (Lemon Squeezy/Paddle, ~5%+50¢, tax handled) only if a single game crosses ~500k plays/month.

| POD (Fourthwall) | **$0/mo**, pay base cost only on a sale [fourthwall.com/faq](https://fourthwall.com/faq) | Per-sale only |

**When Supabase starts charging:** your data shape is microscopic — a score row is ~100 bytes and cloud saves are hard-capped at 32KB (per your `schema.sql`), served through cached views. That's **~5 million score rows before you'd worry about the 500MB cap.** You don't use Supabase Auth, so the 50k "MAU" meter barely applies. **You will not pay Supabase a cent at 10k or 100k monthly players.** The honest caveat: at **~1M players**, if leaderboard reads scale with traffic you could exceed the 50k-MAU / 5GB-egress free limits and need **Pro (US$25/mo)** — but the *game itself* stays $0 on GH Pages/Cloudflare regardless. The two real free-tier risks are the **500MB DB cap** and the **1-week inactivity pause** (a non-issue while the game has daily players). **Break-even = your first ~2 merch sales of the year.**

## Distribution & growth playbook (near-$0, ranked by ROI)

1. **Submit to CrazyGames FIRST (highest-leverage move, $0).** Integrate the SDK, opt into the 2-month launch exclusivity for **+50% share** (costs nothing when you're not on other portals yet), take the homepage-new-games exposure. Distribution — not the product — is your gap; the game is already live, client-side, and mobile-ready. **First, budget the initial download and run Lighthouse** so load time doesn't tank the "first 3 minutes" retention portals select on.
2. **Build an image SCORE-CARD share loop (single biggest virality upgrade, $0).** Your `HUD.ts shareRun` is **text-only today.** Render a canvas card ("Saved Candle Lake · Score 48,210 · 3★ · bucketmyfire.com") and pass it as a *file* to `navigator.share({files})`, with a clipboard-image fallback on desktop and a "Beat my score" leaderboard-challenge link. Social shares are the documented #1 acquisition driver for browser games; you already own the Share button + leaderboard plumbing.
3. **Weekly short-form video engine (over-invest here, ~$0).** This game is *built* for it: a slung bucket missing a swing, a fire re-flaring, a town saved at the last second, golden-hour god-rays. Run a **"fail / near-miss compilation" cadence on TikTok + YouTube Shorts + Reels** — repurpose the same 30-60s clip across all three (1-min mechanic clips beat 15s). A single ~100k-view clip can yield ~1-2% conversion to plays/wishlists [acorngames.gg](https://acorngames.gg/blog/2025/8/10/the-indie-devs-guide-to-mastering-tiktok-in-2025). The clips double as merch-design source material. Track cost-per-result, not views; organic reach is power-law (expect many duds).
4. **Ethical seasonal newsjacking (live right now, $0).** Post educational "how a real bucket drop works vs the game" content, lean into the **Saskatchewan/Canada civic-pride + Candle Lake** angle, and **pledge a visible % of merch profit to a Canadian wildfire-relief / firefighters' fund** (the *This War of Mine* playbook: charity tie-ins earn durable free PR and lift merch conversion [gamesradar.com](https://www.gamesradar.com/this-war-of-mine-raises-dollar500000-for-war-child-charity/)). **Keep it respectful** — pause promo around acute named tragedies; flippancy about real evacuations is reputational poison.
5. **Coordinated launch posts (trivial, $0):** r/WebGames (the documented early-spark channel), plus r/flightsim, r/helicopters, r/Saskatchewan where rules allow, and a **"Show HN: I built a 3D helicopter wildfire game that runs in your browser"** — the "100% client-side, procedural Three.js, zero-backend" story is a genuine hook for that audience. Lead with a GIF, be present in comments.
6. **Google Play TWA wrap (soon, ~CAD $34 one-time).** Your `public/` currently has **no PWA manifest or service worker** — add them, hit Lighthouse PWA ≥80, wrap with the free Bubblewrap CLI, pay the one-time $25 USD Play fee. Gets an install/ASO funnel and a "Get it on Google Play" badge that boosts legitimacy and merch trust. **Do NOT attempt iOS** — Apple rule 4.2.2 rejects web-wrapper games; iPhone players already get the mobile web build [github.com/GoogleChromeLabs/bubblewrap](https://github.com/GoogleChromeLabs/bubblewrap).
7. **Non-exclusive volume portals (soon, $0):** one GameDistribution and/or GameMonetize SDK upload to syndicate across thousands of small sites; keep itch.io + Newgrounds as free validation/devlog homes and enter any itch web-jam for the exposure spike. *Never* alongside a Poki exclusive.
8. **Keep bucketmyfire.com canonical and DECLINE Poki web-exclusivity** until the 8MB/Web-Fit question is resolved and your CrazyGames numbers are in. Exclusivity would sever the own-domain merch funnel and lock out every other channel for 5 years.

**In-game viral/share loops to build (priority order):** (1) image score-card share; (2) leaderboard "beat my score" challenge link; (3) win-screen merch CTA; (4) a tiny optional email capture so you own a fan list portals can't take away.

## Legal & business setup (mandatory minimums)

- **Privacy Policy + Terms of Use + cookie/ad-consent banner** — **mandatory before you monetize.** Use a free generator (TermsFeed/Termly) tuned for PIPEDA + a COPPA clause; ~CAD $0-50.
- **COPPA / PIPEDA is your biggest hidden landmine.** A cartoon firefighting game clearly appeals to under-13s, and you collect email. COPPA applies to services "directed to children under 13" or with actual knowledge they collect personal info from them, requiring verifiable parental consent; PIPEDA says children generally can't validly consent [ftc.gov COPPA FAQ](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/complying-coppa-frequently-asked-questions). **Compliant path:** keep email strictly **optional** (you already do), **never gate play on it**, add a neutral "not directed to children under 13" statement + light age screen, and keep the email hashed (you already SHA-256 it client-side — good).
- **Trademark — rename "Bambi bucket" everywhere player-facing.** It's an active **SEI Industries** trademark with ~95% market share [sei-ind.com/products/bambi-bucket](https://www.sei-ind.com/products/bambi-bucket/). Descriptive in-game use is defensible, but **do NOT put "Bambi Bucket" on merch, in the store, store/portal listings, or ad copy** — use "water bucket" / "fire bucket" / "slung bucket." Similarly, don't imply official affiliation with real agencies, provincial parks, or Bell/Sikorsky on merch. *(Note: your own internal docs flag "water bomber" as the wrong term too — the craft is a helitanker/bucket helicopter; sweep that copy while you're at it.)* Your coined mark **"bucketmyfire" is distinctively protectable** — do a free CIPO clearance search now; defer registration (~CAD $330+) until revenue justifies it.
- **Canadian solo tax reality:** **stay a sole proprietor** — incorporation (~$200+ setup + annual filings/accountant) is pure overhead at this revenue; report business income on your personal T1. **You are NOT required to register for or charge GST/HST until ~CAD $30,000 of taxable revenue over a rolling 4 quarters** (or in a single quarter) [canada.ca GST/HST registration](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/businesses/topics/gst-hst-businesses/when-register-charge.html). Track revenue in a spreadsheet so the threshold doesn't surprise you mid-year.
- **File a W-8BEN before enabling any US ad network** (free, ~10 min, inside the AdSense/network dashboard, valid 3 years) — claims the Canada-US treaty so you keep **~0% US withholding instead of losing 24-30%** [support.google.com/adsense/answer/14131950](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/14131950?hl=en). Fourthwall being Merchant-of-Record means you owe **no US sales-tax filings** for merch.

## KPIs & decision gates

**Instrument now** (plays, sessions, completion %, share-button uses, merch click-through, store conversion) so you measure *your* numbers against the benchmarks — all published figures are platform-wide.

**Measure monthly:**

| KPI | Benchmark / target | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly plays | 50k-300k = "good," 1-5M = breakout [ESTIMATE] | derived above |
| Blended ad Session RPM | ~$1.00-$1.70/1k (anything above = win) | [adpushup](https://www.adpushup.com/blog/HTML5-game-ads/) |
| Day-1 retention | ~24-30% is a healthy casual baseline | industry casual benchmark |
| Win-screen → store CTR | track it; this is the merch lever | — |
| Store conversion | ~1.5-1.7% of *store visitors* | [printify](https://printify.com/blog/most-profitable-print-on-demand-products/) |
| Player → buyer conversion | 0.1-0.3% realistic; 0.5% = strong funnel | [mediacube](https://mediacube.io/en-US/blog/merch-maker-for-youtubers-best-unique-items-for-your-subscribers) [ESTIMATE-adjusted] |

**Go / no-go gates:**
- **DOUBLE DOWN if, by ~Day 90:** CrazyGames is sending sustained traffic (≥50k plays/mo), *and* you've sold ≥10-15 merch units off the win-screen funnel (proves the funnel works, not just the store), *and* at least one short-form clip cleared ~50k+ views. → Expand to 5-7 SKUs, add a second portal non-exclusively, consider the Play wrap push, ramp the video cadence.
- **LET IT RIDE (low-maintenance brand asset) if:** traffic is modest (<20k plays/mo) and merch is trickling. → Keep the $0 store live (it costs nothing), post seasonally around fire season, and let SEO + leaderboard compound. Don't burn scarce part-time hours chasing early sales — the median POD seller takes **~165 days to their first $1,000** [printify POD statistics](https://printify.com/blog/print-on-demand-statistics/).
- **HARD STOPS to avoid:** never sign Poki's 5-year web-exclusive (kills the merch-on-your-domain pillar); never model ads on in-app/$16-rewarded numbers (the corrected web RPM is ~$1-2); never chase iOS; never let merch effort exceed the 3-SKU + win-screen-funnel cap until buy-through is proven. The binding constraint is your solo bandwidth — **doing the top 2 things well (CrazyGames + weekly short-form) beats doing all eight half-heartedly.**
