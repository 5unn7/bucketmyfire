# Design: The Front Door — Repositioning bucketmyfire as a Credible Wildfire Window

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-06-09 · Status: APPROVED · Mode: Startup
Builds on (not superseded): the "Live Wildfire Tracker — the Honest Window" design (the data layer this sits on top of).
Working copy in-repo; canonical original at `~/.gstack/projects/5unn7-bucketmyfire/5unn7-main-design-20260609-114626.md`.

## Problem Statement

The product is shifting its position. It is no longer "a helicopter flight sim (that also
sells merch)." The new position, in the owner's words: *"educating and preparing people along
with government... we should also provide relevant information."* Pressed on what "along with
government" means, the owner clarified it is **not** a government sales relationship — it means
**surface the same authoritative-grade data government publishes** (CIFFC / SPSA / CWFIS, already
wired) woven into something people actually open. Be a credible public wildfire source; the game
is the hook that earns the attention.

The owner's trigger: *"Our title screen surely doesn't justify the position we are going in."*
Today `index.html` boots straight into the heavy Three.js app. The front door is a game splash —
it makes no claim to credibility, carries no real information, and indexes poorly. The artifacts
the owner just hand-built (`mockups/loading-howto-a/b/c.html`) are polished but are 100% "how to
fly the game" — they prove the gap: the position is public wildfire awareness, the surfaces are
all flight-tutorial.

The ask, verbatim: *"Lets plan user flows from titlescreen. We should go back to html or
something else too probably? You tell me."*

## Demand Evidence

Honest read, because this is the load-bearing risk:

- **Validated demand exists for the GAME** — it is live at bucketmyfire.com, has users, a
  leaderboard, repeat play. That is real.
- **Demand for the EDUCATION / PREPAREDNESS position is a BET, not yet behaviorally proven.** No
  user has said "I come to bucketmyfire to know if fires are near me" or "this is where I check
  the season." This session did not surface a named person who needs the *information* layer. The
  closest real signal is the owner's own pull toward it and the already-shipped "Honest Window"
  tracker (prior approved design).
- The wedge that makes the bet cheap: the data and the map are **already built**. The live CIFFC
  summary feed (`api.ciffc.net/v1/dashboard/summary`) and the Leaflet map (`src/three/livefire/`,
  a lazy 152KB chunk decoupled from Three) exist. Repositioning the front door is mostly
  *re-presentation* of assets in hand, not net-new capability.

**This is the #1 thing to validate before over-investing** (see The Assignment). The design is
structured so the bet is cheap to place and the existing game keeps working if the education
angle underperforms.

## Status Quo

What a person does *today* to know about wildfire near them:

- Checks a government dashboard (CIFFC, SPSA, provincial fire maps), firesmoke.ca, or local-news
  fire maps. These are **accurate but cold** — utilitarian, government-grey, built for emergencies,
  not for ambient awareness.
- Or, more commonly: **nothing**, until smoke is on the horizon — then panic-Googles. There is no
  habit, no ambient awareness, no on-ramp to preparedness. The cost of the status quo is exactly
  that: fire is invisible until it is an emergency.

The opening the product can take: be the source you open *because it's compelling*, not because
you're scared — and have the real numbers + the preparedness on-ramp there when curiosity turns to
concern. The competitor is not another game; it is the grey government dashboard and "nothing."

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary audience (owner's pick): a hub serving the concerned/curious public** — a person in or
adjacent to fire country, or a Canadian who lands during fire season. The original pick was a
"co-equal hub," challenged and refined (Premise 2) into a **spine model**: the live fire situation
is the hero, and the three doors are equal *actions* on it — reachable in one tap, but **not** a
flat 3-tile grid. Do not build a three-tile menu.

**Canonical door labels (lock these — earlier drafts drifted):**
- **"Fight the fire"** = the primary action / the game (never "Play" in UI copy).
- **"Live Fires"** = the live map + data.
- **"Prepare"** = the preparedness section.

**Narrowest wedge (smallest shippable version worth shipping this week):** re-shell `index.html`
into an editorial HTML front door that leads with a **live-stat hero** (real CIFFC numbers, now),
renders the **existing Leaflet map**, carries a **Prepare** section that links official sources,
and routes **"Fight the fire"** into the existing game (lazy-loaded). Nothing new is invented — the
data, the map, and the game all exist; this is composition + a load-order change.

## Constraints

- **Client-side only.** No backend except the env-gated Supabase leaderboard. Data stays keyless
  CIFFC/CWFIS (CORS `*`).
- **CIFFC summary is Canada-only** (hidden for US/MX per prior work). Province default =
  Saskatchewan is correct; do not promise a US number you cannot source.
- **Data must degrade gracefully** — off-season (near-zero fires), API-down, and stale-source must
  all have honest fallbacks (carried over from the "Honest Window" trust rules).
- **Mobile 60fps** for the game; one-time gen, O(1)/frame, no shader recompiles (unchanged).
- **No-scroll rule applies to the GAME surfaces, not the front door.** The new HTML front door is a
  *content/editorial page* and is explicitly allowed to scroll (hero → strip → map → prepare →
  footer). The single-viewport hard rule still governs every in-flight HUD and in-game menu.
- **Design system reuse:** warm "fight" register = editorial/brand; cool JetBrains-Mono "instrument"
  register = live data. Front door consumes `theme.ts`/`tokens.css` as-is; no new token object.
- **Auto-deploys on push to `main`** (GitHub Pages). Front door ships on the same static pipeline.

## Premises (all confirmed by owner)

1. The front door's job is **credibility + routing, not gameplay.** The game proves the cause; it
   is not the lobby. (Justifies a light HTML front door over booting the heavy app.)
2. The hub needs a **spine**: the live fire situation is the hero/backdrop; Play / Live / Prepare
   are three equal *actions* on it — **not** a flat 3-tile grid. (Refines the "co-equal hub" pick so
   it reads as a product with a point of view, not a web portal.)
3. **"Prepare" must be real, sourced, authoritative content** (echoes/links FireSmart, SPSA,
   CIFFC), never invented tips. Thin or made-up prep content destroys the credibility position.
4. **The 3D world must not gate first paint.** Front door paints instantly as DOM/HTML; WebGL loads
   only when someone chooses to fly.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Reorder the boot, keep the app
Keep booting `src/three/main.ts`, but render the DOM hub first (reusing the in-app home menus +
Leaflet livefire) and build the 3D world only on "Fight the fire."
- Effort: S (human ~1–2 days / CC ~2 hrs). Risk: Low.
- Pros: smallest diff; reuses everything; no entry restructure.
- Cons: the big Three.js bundle still downloads up front (DOM paints fast but JS payload stays
  heavy); SEO stays weak (SPA); Prepare content lives in JS strings, not indexable. Does not really
  "go back to HTML" — just reorders the boot.

### Approach B: HTML shell, game as a lazy room (RECOMMENDED)
`index.html` becomes the real content-first hub (live-stat hero, live map, prepare). The Three.js
game is code-split and dynamically `import()`-ed only when the user taps "Fight the fire."
- Effort: M, in **two slices** (see below). Slice B1 (additive hub) human ~3–5 days / CC ~half a
  day; Slice B2 (home-hub reconciliation) human ~2–4 days / CC ~half a day. Risk: Med (B2 carries
  regression risk to the recently-built Province / Open Skies / Solo entry).
- Pros: instant paint; tiny initial JS; info-seekers never download the game bundle; much stronger
  SEO/credibility; directly justifies the position. The Leaflet map is already a Three-decoupled
  lazy chunk, so it renders in the HTML shell with no Three dependency. Structured so Prepare
  topics can graduate to standalone indexed pages later without a rewrite.
- Cons: requires splitting the `main.ts` entry into hub-shell + game-module, a small router, and a
  clean DOM-hub → 3D handoff; the in-app home hub's role must be reconciled (see Open Questions).

### Approach C: Multi-page static + the app
Separate real URLs: `/` (hub), `/prepare` (+ per-topic pages), `/live`, and the game as its own
entry like `shop.html` already is.
- Effort: M–L (human ~1–2 weeks / CC ~1–2 days). Risk: Med.
- Pros: best SEO/authority — "how do helicopters fight fires", "SK fire today", "what is FWI"
  become indexable pages that compound the education position over time; each page shareable.
- Cons: most surface to build and keep in sync; nav/shell duplication across Vite entries.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B, structured with a C on-ramp.** Ship the HTML shell now; design Prepare so its topics
can be promoted to standalone indexed pages (C) incrementally when the content justifies the SEO
investment. B is the honest answer to "go back to HTML?": yes for the *front door*, while the 3D
stays a destination you choose to enter.

**Entry topology (lock this for the implementer):** the served `index.html` carries **hand-authored
static markup** for the hero (brand, fallback headline copy, subtitle, CTA, `<title>`/meta) so a
crawler and a cold first paint both see real words with zero JS. It boots a light `src/hub.ts` (not
`main.ts`) that hydrates the live number onto the static hero, mounts the Leaflet map, and wires
the doors. **"Fight the fire" does `import('./three/main')`** to pull the game bundle on demand.
The game keeps the `shop.html`-style separation in spirit, but stays a lazy module of the same SPA
(not a second HTML entry) so the hub→game handoff is in-process.

**Build it as two slices to contain the regression risk:**
- **Slice B1 (additive, low-risk):** new static `index.html` + `src/hub.ts` + the editorial hero +
  live strip + Leaflet map + Prepare. "Fight the fire" lazy-imports the *existing* `main.ts` and
  lands on the *existing* in-app home exactly as today. Nothing in the game changes. Shippable alone.
- **Slice B2 (reconciliation, higher-risk, separable):** demote `ui/home/menus.ts` from "home" to
  an in-game pause/menu so "Fight the fire" drops straight into the flying-surface picker (Province
  / Open Skies / Solo). This touches recent entry code — do it as its own reviewed change behind B1.

### Front-door layout (editorial composition, terminal spine)

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  [flame mark]  BUCKET MY FIRE              About · Shop  │  ← thin brand bar
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                          │
│   HERO  (warm / editorial, over boreal photo)           │
│   "There are ~{N} active fires in Saskatchewan          │
│    and {M} burning across Canada right now."            │
│   Experience how helicopters fight fires.               │
│   [ FIGHT THE FIRE ]                                     │
│                                                          │
│   ── live data strip (cool / JetBrains Mono) ──         │
│   {N} active · {ha} ha · OC {x} BH {y} UC {z} · FWI {f} │
│   updated {Xm} ago · source CIFFC          ● live       │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   LIVE FIRE MAP  (existing Leaflet, interactive)        │  ← above/at fold
│   tap a fire → the real 35-field record (already built) │
│   inline CTA: "See it from the air → Fight the fire"    │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   PREPARE  (editorial cards, below the fold)            │
│   Know your risk · Make a plan · Build a go-bag         │
│   each → official source (FireSmart / SPSA / CIFFC)     │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   The cause + Wear the fight (→ /shop.html)             │
│   "Not for emergency decisions — see official sources." │  ← honest disclaimer
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Hero number is the spine** (Premise 2): it makes the credibility claim literally true on
  landing, and it earns "Fight the fire" (the keystone brand line).
- **Two registers map to two existing token sets**: warm hero/cards = the fight; mono live strip +
  map chrome = the instrument. No new tokens.
- **Season-aware hero** (mandatory): when the live national count is low/zero (off-season), the
  hero must never say "0 fires active." **v1 fallback = the preparedness-forward line** ("Fire
  season returns in spring. Know your risk before it does." → Prepare) because it needs **no new
  data source**. The retrospective line ("Last season, {X} fires burned {Y} hectares in
  Saskatchewan") is **v2-only and gated on confirming a keyless+CORS historical source** — it is a
  *second* data dependency and must not be smuggled into the "nothing new is invented" wedge.
- **Map states inside the hub**: the embedded Leaflet map carries the "Honest Window" degradation
  rules locally — a skeleton/placeholder while the 152KB chunk and tiles load, and an honest "map
  unavailable — see official sources" panel (with the outbound link) if tiles or the feed fail.
  Never a blank rectangle.
- **Brand bar links**: "Shop" → `/shop.html` (existing). "About" → a short anchor section in the
  footer (cause + "not for emergencies" + attribution), not a new page in v1.

### User-flow map (from the front door)

1. **Fight the fire (primary)** → lazy-load the 3D game → flying surface (Province / Open Skies /
   Solo) → play → end screen → return. **Return contract:** a return to the front door is a **page
   reload** (back to `index.html`), consistent with the existing "mission switch = page reload"
   invariant — the game does not try to tear down WebGL and re-mount the HTML shell in place. Share
   and Shop are reachable from the end screen without leaving the game.
2. **Live Fire Map** → pan/zoom → tap a fire → real record → cross-link "See it from the air →
   Fight the fire" (ties the data back to the game).
3. **Prepare** → topic card → official source (outbound v1) → (future C: internal indexed page).
4. **Cause / Wear the fight** → `/shop.html` (existing same-tab UTM funnel).
5. **Profile / Leaderboard** → secondary, reachable from the flying surface or a hub link.

## Open Questions

1. **In-app home hub vs HTML front door (biggest integration question).** The game's current
   `ui/home/menus.ts` is a second "home." Under B, does the HTML front door *become* the only home
   (tapping Fight the fire drops straight into the flying-surface picker), with the in-game home
   demoted to a pause/menu? Recommended yes, but it touches the Province/Open Skies/Solo entry flow
   from recent work — needs an eng-review pass.
2. **Province detection** for the hero: default SK, geo-IP, or a small picker? Default SK is the
   safe v1; revisit if non-SK traffic grows.
3. **SEO for a JS-injected hero — RESOLVED for v1: hand-authored static markup.** No prerender
   tooling. The hero's brand, fallback headline, subtitle, and CTA live as literal HTML in
   `index.html`; `hub.ts` hydrates the live number on top. This keeps the client-side-only
   constraint and makes the v1 "real indexable document" criterion buildable. A build-time prerender
   step is only reconsidered alongside the C on-ramp (per-topic Prepare pages).
4. **When does Prepare graduate to indexed pages (C)?** Trigger = first evidence anyone searches for
   the info. Measurement uses the **Cloudflare Web Analytics already auto-injected at the edge** (the
   domain is Cloudflare-proxied per `index.html`, even though hosting is GitHub Pages — not a
   mismatch); add no manual beacon (it would double-count).
5. **Merch placement** in the hub — footer-only (current sketch) or a stronger cause moment? Defer
   to the apparel-led north-star plan.

## Success Criteria

- First contentful paint < ~1.5s on a mid-tier phone; the **game bundle is not downloaded** unless
  "Fight the fire" is tapped (verifiable in the network panel).
- The hero renders a **true live number** in season **and** a **true fallback** off-season; never a
  spinner, blank, or "0 fires."
- Every Prepare link resolves to a real official source; nothing invented.
- All three doors (Fight the fire / Live Fires / Prepare) reachable in one tap from the front door.
- Front door is a real indexable document (crawler sees hero fallback text, title, description).
- No regression to the existing game entry, leaderboard, or shop funnel.

## Distribution Plan

Existing GitHub Pages auto-deploy on push to `main` covers it — the front door is the same static
site (`base: './'`). One addition for the SEO/credibility goal: ensure the hero's static fallback
copy is present in the served HTML (static markup) so the page indexes as a real document, not an
empty SPA shell. No new pipeline.

## Dependencies

- **The "Honest Window" tracker design** is the data/trust layer this front door stands on — its
  degradation rules, freshness labeling, and "not for emergencies" disclaimer carry straight up to
  the hero + strip.
- **CIFFC summary must expose the numbers the hero needs** (national active count + a Saskatchewan
  count + a real freshness timestamp). If SK is not in the summary payload, the province number
  needs a second source. **This is what The Assignment de-risks.**

## The Assignment (do this before building)

Before designing a single pixel further: **pull `api.ciffc.net/v1/dashboard/summary` by hand right
now** and confirm it returns (a) a live national active-fire count, (b) a per-province Saskatchewan
count, and (c) a real "last updated" timestamp. If SK isn't in that payload, your hero's province
number needs a different source — find it today.

Then, with the real numbers in front of you, **hand-write both hero lines and read them aloud**:
the in-season live version against today's actual numbers, and the v1 off-season *preparedness-
forward* fallback (no historical data needed). If the live line doesn't punch and the off-season
line doesn't hold on its own, the front-door premise is wrong — and you'll know it before you build,
for the cost of ten minutes.

## A note on the three loading mockups

`mockups/loading-howto-a/b/c.html` are **not** the front door — they are the loading / how-to screen
that plays *while the game bundle lazy-loads* after someone taps "Fight the fire." The front door
teaches the *position*; these teach the *controls*, at the transition into the game. Of the three,
**C (split hero + controls panel)** reads most like the credible-instrument register; **A** is the
most cinematic for a film cold-open feel.
